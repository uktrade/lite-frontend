The government classifies information assets to ensure they're appropriately protected.

Guidance on government security classifications (opens in a new tab).

The classification can include additional markings, such as:

  • prefix, like UK, NATO or OCCAR
  • handling instructions, like RECIPIENTS ONLY, FOR PUBLIC RELEASE or HMG USE ONLY
  • descriptors, like PERSONAL DATA, MARKET SENSITIVE, or COMMERCIAL
  • codewords, which are most commonly applied to SECRET and TOP SECRET assets and are single words in CAPITAL letters
  • national caveats, like UK EYES ONLY or FIVE EYES ONLY
Prefixes are listed before the security classification.

Handling instructions, descriptions, codewords or national caveats are listed after the security classification.
